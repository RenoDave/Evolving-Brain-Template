# DECISION MAKING PRINCIPLES

> The rules and filters you run every decision through.

## Core principles

- **Benefit filter first** — when torn between options, ask: "what will benefit me and what I'm trying to accomplish?" Not what feels easier, not what other people think. What moves the goal forward?
- **Experience over instinct in new territory** — trust your gut hard in areas where you have deep experience (construction, carpentry, trade). In new domains (business, AI, marketing), override the gut and learn before committing.
- **No regrets by design** — every decision was the right one with the information available at the time. This isn't denial — it's refusing to let past decisions drain energy that belongs to the present.

## Heuristics I trust

- If I've done it 10,000 hours, my gut is reliable. If I haven't, it isn't yet.
- The environment shapes the habit more than willpower does. Change the environment first.
- Success funds freedom; freedom funds growth. Sequence matters — don't try to skip steps.

## Biases I watch for

- **Conflict avoidance** — tendency to sidestep friction instead of addressing it directly. Watch for this in business negotiations, team conversations, pricing.
- **Paralysis from overwhelm** — when the gap between where I am and where I want to be feels too wide, I can freeze instead of act. Antidote: pick the smallest next step and just do that.
- **Environment dependency** — routines that depend on other people's buy-in tend to collapse. Build habits that don't require anyone else to show up.
