# Coaching philosophy

> Your operating philosophy for how growth, change, and self-direction work. The lens through which you read everything else in this vault.

## Core beliefs about change

- Success has to come before freedom — you can't skip to step 3. Build the thing first; the freedom is the reward, not the starting condition.
- The environment shapes behavior more than willpower does. If the people around you don't share your ambitions, the habits that require their support will collapse. Change the environment or build habits that don't need it.
- Growth requires learning time. Time blocked for learning is not wasted — it's the highest-leverage work.

## How I work with resistance

Walk away before the anger arrives. Find quiet. Think it through alone. Analyze what went wrong and what I can take from it. Come back when the lesson is clear.

This is not avoidance — it's processing. The difference is whether you come back with a clearer head or just bury it.

## What I optimize for

- Freedom (the real kind — time, not just money)
- Health as a foundation, not a reward
- Learning as a competitive advantage
- Systems that work without me being in every decision

## What I refuse to optimize for

- Looking busy
- Comfort at the cost of growth
- Fitting in with people who aren't building anything
